From July 2013 until June of the following year, I helped develop CAPITAL: Comprehension and Pronunciation Instructional Tools for Adult Learners. It is a collection of automated tutoring tools for instructors and students being developed by the Learning Technologies Research (LeTR) Group at The George Washington University. The group's motivation is to tackle the adult literacy problem through the development of widely-available mobile technologies for literacy instruction and assessment. They seek to automate the generation of exercises in order to tailor exercises to the needs of every individual student. CAPITAL Words, my project, specifically targets illiterate and low-literacy adults, focusing on phonemic awareness and pronunciation skills as a means of improving students’ reading abilities.

In June 2013, while studying abroad at Korea University, I started developing ManyTones, a relatively simple Android application that automatically shuffles users’ favorite ringtones, notification alerts and/or alarm sounds each time they get a phone call, receive a notification or their alarm goes off, respectively. This effectively allows users to have multiple tones of their choosing on their Android devices instead of being locked into one for each tone type. Subsequent updates also allow users to easily convert any sounds on their device into Android-compatible tones for use with ManyTones, Android’s native tone system or any other tone-related apps. While ManyTones and all its features are available for free on Google Play, an optional donate version is also available.

From about March 2008 to August 2012, I developed and maintained various modifications of computer games built on the Source game engine as co-founder, lead programmer and website designer/manager of the now-defunct Agent Red Productions.
